Quick Meals for New Dads: 10-Minute Food That Actually Fills You Up

By Tom | February 2026 | 5 min read

when you're running on 4 hours of sleep, cooking a proper meal feels impossible. so you eat toast. then more toast. then whatever biscuits are in the cupboard. then you feel terrible and your energy crashes harder.

I lived on toast and cereal for the first month. it was not great. these days I've got a rotation of meals that take under 10 minutes, need almost no cooking skill, and actually keep you going for a few hours.

The rules

every meal here follows three rules:

  • under 10 minutes from start to eating. if it takes longer, you won't make it when the baby is screaming.
  • one hand optional. most of these can be eaten with one hand while holding a baby with the other. this matters more than you'd think.
  • protein + complex carbs. this combo keeps blood sugar stable and energy consistent. toast on its own spikes and crashes. toast with eggs doesn't.

Quick meals that actually work

Eggs on toast (the upgraded version)

5 minutes

scramble 2-3 eggs with a bit of butter. pile onto wholemeal toast. add whatever's in the fridge: cheese, leftover veg, hot sauce. this is probably 60% of my meals as a new dad and it never gets old.

the protein from eggs keeps you full for 3-4 hours. the carbs from bread give you immediate energy. it works.

Greek yoghurt bowl

3 minutes

big bowl of full-fat Greek yoghurt. handful of granola or oats. banana sliced on top. drizzle of honey if you want.

this has more protein than you'd expect (about 15-20g depending on the yoghurt). good for breakfast or a 3pm snack when you're fading. can eat it one-handed with a spoon.

Microwave quesadilla

4 minutes

tortilla, grated cheese, whatever leftover protein you have (chicken, beans, even last night's mince). fold in half, microwave for 90 seconds. not as good as a pan-fried one, but you're not entering a cooking competition. you're surviving.

Peanut butter banana wrap

2 minutes

tortilla, thick layer of peanut butter, sliced banana, roll it up. this is my go-to 2am meal when the baby's had a rough night. no cooking, no dishes (eat it over the sink if you want), and the fat + carbs keep you going.

Tin of beans on toast

5 minutes

the most British meal possible, and also secretly decent nutrition. beans have about 10g of protein per tin plus fibre. add cheese on top. it's not Instagram food but it's solid fuel when you're exhausted.

the goal isn't to eat perfectly. it's to eat something better than biscuits and cereal. even small upgrades (adding an egg, swapping white bread for wholemeal) make a noticeable difference to your energy levels when you're sleep deprived.

Batch cooking for the desperate

if you can find one hour on a weekend (ask your partner, ask a grandparent, ask anyone), you can make a batch of something that lasts the week. the best options for new dads:

  • chilli or bolognese. make a huge pot. freeze half in portions. microwave in 3 minutes. rice or pasta on the side. this is probably the most efficient meal you can make.
  • overnight oats. mix oats, milk, yoghurt, and whatever toppings in jars the night before. grab one from the fridge in the morning. zero effort when you need it most.
  • hard-boiled eggs. boil a dozen on Sunday. keep in the fridge. grab 2-3 whenever you need protein. they last about a week.

What to keep stocked

the reason new dads eat badly isn't laziness. it's that there's nothing quick in the house. keep these basics stocked and you'll always have a 5-minute meal available:

  • eggs (the single most useful food item)
  • bread (wholemeal lasts longer)
  • peanut butter
  • bananas
  • Greek yoghurt
  • tinned beans
  • cheese
  • tortillas

total weekly shop for this list: about fifteen quid. not bad for a week of meals that'll keep you functional.

if you're finding that you're not eating at all, or only eating once a day, that's worth paying attention to. loss of appetite combined with sleep deprivation and low mood can be a sign of something more than just being busy. talk to someone about it.
